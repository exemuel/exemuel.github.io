Introduction

Genealogy (tarombo) occupies a central role in Batak culture. It preserves family history, documents ancestral relationships, and strengthens the identity shared by members of the same clan (marga). This page presents a portion of my family's genealogy within the Situmeang clan and traces the lineage across fourteen generations.

The Origin of the Situmeang Clan

The Situmeang clan (Surat Batak: ᯘᯪᯖᯮᯔᯩᯀᯰ) is one of the descendant clans of Naipospos. According to Batak genealogical tradition, the clan originated from Jamita Mangaraja, the fourth son of Naipospos. The Situmeang lineage belongs to the larger Naipospos family, alongside the Sibagariang, Hutauruk, Simanungkalit, Marbun Lumbanbatu, Marbun Banjarnahor, and Marbun Lumbangaol clans.

Naipospos had sons from two wives. His eldest son, Donda Hopol (ancestor of the Sibagariang clan), was born to his first wife, followed by Marbun, who was born to his second wife. Three additional sons were later born to the first wife: Donda Ujung (ancestor of Hutauruk), Ujung Tinumpak (ancestor of Simanungkalit), and Jamita Mangaraja (ancestor of Situmeang).

Although Marbun was the second son by birth order, Batak tarombo traditionally groups descendants according to maternal lineage. Consequently, genealogical records usually list the descendants of the first wife before those of the second wife whenever an ancestor had children from multiple marriages.
